Project Analysis for LucyStore.com

Created by Jess Marinello

November 1, 2013

Client: Everything Imagination, LLC

Site Intent

Client Goals

Redesign site with a more direct e-Commerce focus to increase sales and provide a rich shopping experience. Particular attention to the home page, including information and products organization, and site navigation is foremost. E-Commerce design should combine a look and usability that is at once unique and eye-catching. Website needs to be as usable and as customer-friendly as possible. Additionally, responsive web design needs to be incorporated for mobile devices. Lastly, W3C validation checks for both HTML5 and CSS contain numerous errors that will be evaluated and corrected accordingly.

Currently, LucyStore.com has a site that is pleasing to the eye, but lacks cohesiveness and direction throughout the site. LucyStore.com offers a plethora of products and information, but finding and navigating to the aforementioned can be time consuming and confusing to the user. While LucyStore.com does offer detailed product information and quality product images, it is lacking in clear design (it is a catchy website design, but there is not a strong enough focus on the products); user-friendly navigation/convenient menus; a simple and transparent checkout process (users are directed to a different page that leaves behind any design of LucyStore.com); adequate product filters and search options; and information regarding payment options.

Consumer Goals

Consumers want the shopping process to be pleasant, quick, and most importantly easy. LucyStore.com accomplishes some of this. Call to action buttons, i.e. "Add to Cart", are well placed and easily distinguishable on product's page. However, more attention needs to be applied to organization and navigation, as well as the checkout page.

Specific Ideas for Redesign

  • Show the advantages of LucyStore.com products. A list of product categories or a grid to showcase products. Special deals and new arrivals are also good to coax the average visitor to buy.
  • Deals, Freebies, and Promos. Grab user interest in a matter of seconds upon their entry to the site; adding shopping deals attracts buyers fast. Discounts and special deals are usually the first things that website visitors look for. Alluring promises and unique prices stimulate visitors to stay on the site, spend money, and come back. Position eye-catching banners with discount offers on the upper part of the home page.
  • Latest news and most popular products. When there are news, a sales period or upcoming events that buyers should know about the product, the home page is the best place to house them. Do not make it too hard for users to find exclusive deals and prices.
  • Payment Systems Icons. Online stores commonly use credit and debit cards, gift vouchers, PayPal, etc as their main payment system. Clarify payment options in advance. Payment details should be displayed on the home page in plain sight and are most easily found at the footer or on the top right corner.
  • Shopping Cart, Login Box and Search Box. The shopping cart, login box and search box features are usually placed together at the top-right of the page.
  • Social Media Links. Social media websites, such as Facebook, Twitter, and Pinterest bring excellent opportunities for self-promotion and also keep customers aware of the latest news, or special deals which are oriented towards followers only. Online communities can also help find new customers who are not familiar with the site yet.
